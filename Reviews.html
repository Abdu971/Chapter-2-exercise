Reviews

Season 1:

Critical Response: The first season received a 92% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes, with an average rating of 7.3/10 based on 75 critic reviews. The consensus reads: "Reacher captures the trademark bulk of its titular hero while trading away some of his definition, but fans of the novels will find plenty to love about this faithful adaptation." - ROTTEN TOMATOES

Audience Reaction: Viewers praised the series for its pacing and faithfulness to the source material. One user commented, "It's one of the best-paced series I've ever seen. You never get bored." - REDDIT


Season 2:

Critical Response: The second season holds a 98% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes, based on 43 critic reviews, with an average rating of 7.8/10. The consensus states: "Brawny as Alan Ritchson's biceps, Reacher swaggers confidently into its sophomore season as rock 'em sock 'em pulp with a sly wink." - ROTTEN TOMATOES

Diverse Opinions: While many critics praised the season, some reviews were less favorable. Forbes described it as "one of the worst shows on TV," suggesting that the protagonist lacked depth. - EN.WIKIPEDIA.ORG


Season 3:

Critical Response: Early reviews indicate that Season 3 continues the series' trend of delivering action-packed entertainment. Decider recommends streaming the new season, highlighting its engaging and entertaining narrative. - DECIDER.COM

Author's Perspective: Lee Child, the author of the "Reacher" series, praised the TV adaptation's improvements over his original books, particularly the addition of Frances Neagley, a character not present in the original "Persuader" novel. - ENTERTAINMENT WEEKLY

Overall, "Reacher" has been well-received, with particular acclaim for its faithful adaptation of the source material and the performance of Alan Ritchson in the titular role. - The Guardian
